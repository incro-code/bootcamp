What AI is good for in finance — and what it isn't
✓ Where Claude genuinely helps
  • ✦ Drafting variance commentary and management narratives in seconds
  • ✦ Turning a P&L table into a sharp 200-word CEO update
  • ✦ Building dynamic Excel models from written instructions
  • ✦ Synthesising long documents: contracts, reports, board packs
  • ✦ Spotting patterns across large datasets when given structured input
  • ✦ Generating first-draft forecasting assumptions from historical context
  • ✦ Answering "what does this covenant actually mean?" on debt documents
✗ Where AI cannot be trusted unsupervised
  • ✦ Accessing live financial data without a verified connector in place
  • ✦ Producing audit-ready outputs — everything needs human sign-off
  • ✦ Regulatory filings, statutory accounts, or tax submissions
  • ✦ Precise arithmetic on large numbers — always validate calculations
  • ✦ Making judgment calls under genuine ambiguity
  • ✦ Replacing domain expertise — it accelerates experts, not beginners
  • ✦ Any task involving raw confidential client data without sanitisation
